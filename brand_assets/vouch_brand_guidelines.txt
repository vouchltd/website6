================================================================
VOUCH LTD. — BRAND GUIDELINES
Google Business Profile Optimization & Review Management
Long Beach, California
================================================================

Last updated: July 2026


1. WHO WE ARE
----------------------------------------------------------------
Vouch Ltd. helps local small businesses in Long Beach, CA get
found, get chosen, and get reviewed. We optimize Google Business
Profiles and manage customer reviews using smart automation, so
owners can focus on running their business instead of fighting
with their online presence.

We are built for owners who are skeptical of agencies that
overpromise. Everything about our brand should feel credible,
grounded, and straightforward.


2. WHAT WE DO
----------------------------------------------------------------
Core services:
- Google Business Profile setup, cleanup, and optimization
- Ongoing profile management (photos, posts, Q&A, categories)
- Review management: monitoring, professional responses, and
  ethical review-generation systems

Pricing model:
- One-time setup fee (profile audit + full optimization)
- Recurring monthly management fee (ongoing upkeep + reviews)

Behind the scenes, the work is powered by an AI agent system
(prospecting and review-response automation). IMPORTANT: this
infrastructure stays internal. Client-facing materials talk about
benefits and results, never about the tech stack.


3. WHO WE SERVE
----------------------------------------------------------------
Local small business owners in Long Beach and surrounding areas:
restaurants, gyms, salons, auto shops, trades, and other
brick-and-mortar or service businesses.

They are busy, practical, and often not tech-savvy. Many have
been burned by marketing agencies before. They respond to plain
language, honest math (star ratings, review counts, search
visibility), and proof — not jargon or hype.


4. BRAND VALUES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Trust first. The name is Vouch for a reason — we vouch for our
  clients, and our conduct should make clients vouch for us.
- No overpromising. We never guarantee specific results in
  contracts or conversations.
- Respect for security. We NEVER ask clients for passwords.
  Access is granted through the Google Business Profile Manager
  role only. This is a hard rule and a selling point.
- Sell honestly, build reliably. Value is proven before it is
  automated.


5. COLOR PALETTE
----------------------------------------------------------------
The palette is strictly alpine green + charcoal + warm clay/ochre.
NO purple. NO blue. NO gradients of either. This is a hard rule
across all materials — print, web, decks, and documents.

PRIMARY
  Alpine Green   #2D5F3F   Headers, CTAs, primary brand color
  Charcoal       #2A2D2E   Body text, banners, dark surfaces
                            (used in place of navy/black)

SECONDARY
  Sage           #5C9C6B   Lighter green support tone

WARM ACCENTS (use one at a time, never both in the same element)
  Clay           #A6432E   Issue callouts, urgent highlights
                            (used instead of a stock red)
  Ochre          #A87F2A   Borderline/amber states, warm
                            highlight moments

BACKGROUND
  Warm paper tone rather than clinical white. Documents and web
  surfaces should feel warm and editorial, not sterile.


6. TYPOGRAPHY
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Display / headlines:   Fraunces (serif — editorial, premium)
  Body text:             Archivo (clean modern sans-serif)
  Labels / data / mono:  Spline Sans Mono

Type should be easy to read at small sizes (business cards,
website headers, signage). Keep hierarchy simple: one display
face, one body face, mono for numbers and labels only.


7. LOGO
----------------------------------------------------------------
Status: final logo direction not yet selected. Two design rounds
(15 concepts total) exist in /mnt/user-data/outputs/vouch_logos/.

Requirements for the final mark, whichever is chosen:
- Icon + wordmark combination; icon must work standalone as a
  32px favicon/app icon
- Must reproduce cleanly in single-color/black (one-ink print)
- No gradients or fine detail that breaks down at small sizes
- Square-ish composition, transparent or white background
- Avoid literal clip-art tropes (generic map-pin-with-star)

Concept themes explored so far: verification marks (pin-check,
shield/seal), local presence (storefront, anchor), growth
(sprout, peak, upward mark), and connection (linked rings,
signal rings).


8. VOICE & TONE
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Plain, confident, and warm. Write like a sharp local
  professional, not a corporate marketer.
- Benefit-led: "more calls, better reviews, easier to find" —
  never "AI-powered n8n workflow automation."
- Honest about limits. No fake urgency, no inflated claims, no
  publishing placeholder results as if they were real data.
- Numbers over adjectives. A star-rating gap stated plainly
  ("you're at 3.8; the businesses winning your customers are at
  4.6+") beats any slogan.
- Natural prose over bullet spam in client-facing writing.


9. DESIGN PRINCIPLES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Editorial restraint. Clean typographic blocks, generous
  spacing, disciplined color use.
- One warm accent per element — clay OR ochre, never both.
- Documents open with a real moment (cover page with the seal
  mark), not a wall of data.
- Motion (on the website) should feel premium and purposeful,
  and always respect reduced-motion accessibility preferences.
- Avoid default "AI agency" aesthetics: blue/purple glow,
  acid-accent-on-black, generic gradient meshes.


10. HARD RULES (NEVER BREAK)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. No purple or blue anywhere in brand materials.
2. Never ask clients for passwords — Manager-role access only.
3. No guaranteed results in agreements or marketing.
4. Backend/AI infrastructure stays off client-facing materials.
5. Placeholder stats never ship as real claims.
6. Clay and ochre never appear together in the same element.

================================================================
Questions about applying these guidelines? Keep it simple: if it
looks hyped, cold, or techy — it's off-brand. If it looks honest,
warm, and sharp — it's Vouch.
